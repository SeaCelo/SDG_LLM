Introduction

In recent years, there has been an increasing focus on achieving sustainable development by addressing global challenges faced by all countries, regardless of their level of development. One of the most critical of these challenges is the need to ensure clean water and sanitation for all. As the Development Bank of Latin America and the Caribbean (known as CAF), we have undertaken a comprehensive analysis of the current state of Sustainable Development Goal 6 (SDG 6), which aims to ensure the availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all.

Contextual Background

Latin America and the Caribbean region, home to nearly 650 million people, faces significant water and sanitation challenges. Despite substantial progress in recent years, a considerable portion of the population still lacks access to clean water and adequate sanitation facilities. Furthermore, escalating climate change impacts, rapid urbanization, and inefficient water management exacerbate these challenges. Addressing SDG 6 is crucial for the region's sustainable development, as it directly impacts health, education, social equity, economic growth, and environmental preservation.

Water is a fundamental resource that underpins human existence and plays a critical role in sustainable development. Universal and equitable access to water is essential for the well-being of people and the prosperity of economies. However, approximately 2.2 billion people still lack access to safely managed drinking water services, making it imperative to address water-related challenges. In response to this urgent need, the United Nations Industrial Development Organization (UNIDO) proudly presents this detailed analytical report focused on Sustainable Development Goal 6 (SDG 6): "Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all."

The primary aim of SDG 6 is to ensure access to water and sanitation for all while sustaining and managing water resources efficiently. It recognizes that access to water and sanitation services are foundational to multiple dimensions of human development, including health, education, gender equality, and economic growth. This analysis aims to delve deeper into the efforts being made to achieve SDG 6 and the challenges that still remain.

Ensuring the availability and sustainable management of water resources requires both quantitative and qualitative considerations. It requires not only increasing water availability, but also safeguarding water quality and ecosystems. This comprehensive approach requires addressing water scarcity, reducing pollution, improving water use efficiency, and promoting integrated water resources management.

One of the key challenges in achieving SDG 6 lies in addressing the multiple dimensions of water-related challenges across countries. Many regions, particularly in developing countries, face water scarcity due to factors such as population growth, climate change, and inefficient water management practices. This scarcity exacerbates conflicts over water resources, poses risks to food security, and leads to economic losses.

Integrated water resources management (IWRM) is a pivotal approach towards overcoming these challenges. It recognizes the interconnectedness of water resources, takes into account social, economic, and environmental aspects, and aims to balance competing water uses to ensure equitable distribution. For effective IWRM, collaboration and coordination among multiple stakeholders, including governments, institutions, and communities, are crucial.

Access to safe drinking water and sanitation is a basic human right according to the United Nations General Assembly. Despite significant progress, around 785 million people still lack even basic drinking water services, and billions still lack access to safely managed sanitation facilities. Developing countries face substantial challenges in providing access to water and sanitation for all, primarily due to financial constraints, weak infrastructure, and lack of awareness.

Another key issue that must be addressed is water pollution. Industrial activities, agricultural practices, and inadequate sanitation systems contribute to the contamination of water sources. This contamination poses severe risks to human health, ecosystems, and economic activities. Prompt action is needed to reduce pollution and ensure the availability of clean water resources.

Innovation and technological advancements play a crucial role in achieving SDG 6. Enhanced monitoring systems, smart water management technologies, and water recycling and treatment methods can lead to more efficient and sustainable water management. Encouraging investment in water infrastructure, promoting research and development, and empowering local communities through capacity building are critical aspects of this process.

International cooperation is vital for realizing SDG 6. Recognizing the need for global efforts towards sustainable water management, UNIDO works closely with governments, international organizations, and other stakeholders to integrate water-related initiatives into national policies and development plans. This cooperation facilitates knowledge-sharing, enhances capacity building, and encourages investment in water-related projects in developing countries.

Collaborative efforts, both on national and international levels, must be further strengthened to overcome existing barriers and ensure universal access to clean water and sanitation services. UNIDO remains firmly committed to supporting countries in their pursuit of SDG 6, recognizing the vital role that water plays in both societal well-being and sustainable development.

Introduction:

The eradication of poverty remains a significant global challenge and is the first goal out of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) established by the United Nations (UN) in 2015. As an expert agency in relief and development, All We Can Methodist recognizes the critical importance of identifying progress, challenges, and opportunities in the achievement of SDG 1 - No Poverty. 

Overview of Progress:

1.1. Global Commitment: Multiple stakeholders, including governments, civil society organizations, and the private sector, have joined hands to address poverty. Reflecting this commitment, the percentage of people living in extreme poverty (measured by incomes of $1.90 per day) reduced from 36% in 1990 to 9.2% in 2017.

1.2. Poverty Reduction: Remarkable progress has been made in areas such as East Asia, Southeast Asia, and Latin America, resulting in millions of individuals being lifted out of extreme poverty. Success stories from countries like China, India, and Vietnam demonstrate the effectiveness of inclusive growth strategies and social assistance programs.

1.3. Access to Basic Services: Improved access to education, healthcare, clean water, and sanitation has benefited millions of impoverished individuals. Enrollment in primary education has increased, particularly in Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia, and South Asia.

1.4. Vulnerable Groups: Social protection programs have made strides in addressing the needs of vulnerable populations, including children, older persons, and people with disabilities. Increased investments in child protection, health insurance, and income support systems have led to positive outcomes.

Challenges and Opportunities:

1. A Persistent Ongoing Challenge: Despite progress, persistent poverty continues to plague certain regions and populations. Sub-Saharan Africa remains the region with the highest poverty rates, and numerous countries in conflict or affected by natural disasters struggle to combat poverty due to ongoing instability.

2. Rising Inequality: Income inequality has been a major barrier to poverty reduction. While significant strides have been made, income disparities between the richest and poorest continue to widen, often exacerbated by inefficiencies, corruption, and lack of social welfare systems.

3. Climate Change and Poverty: Environmental degradation, climate change impacts, and extreme weather events disproportionately affect impoverished communities, undermining their livelihoods and exacerbating inequalities. Innovative approaches that integrate climate resilience and poverty reduction are essential.

4. Conflict and Fragility: Countries facing conflict, political instability, or weak governance face unique challenges in poverty eradication. Protracted crises hinder sustainable development efforts, requiring a tailored approach that blends humanitarian assistance with long-term development strategies.

5. Data Gaps and Monitoring: Despite improvements in data collection, significant gaps persist, particularly in fragile contexts. Precise poverty data disaggregated by age, gender, disability status, and other social factors are necessary for targeted interventions and informed policy-making.

6. Mobilizing Resources: The financing gap hampers progress towards SDG 1. Mobilizing domestic resources, improving international assistance, exploring innovative financing tools, and facilitating private sector engagement are critical for scaling up poverty eradication efforts.

7. Leave No One Behind: Although progress has been made, marginalized groups including indigenous peoples, ethnic minorities, refugees, and migrants are often disproportionately affected by poverty and face systemic challenges. Targeted strategies are required to ensure their inclusion.

Conclusion:

The journey towards achieving SDG 1 - No Poverty is multi-faceted and requires concerted efforts from all stakeholders involved. While significant progress has been made in poverty reduction and access to basic services, persistent challenges such as rising inequality and the impact of climate change need to be urgently addressed. Governments, international organizations, and civil society must commit to inclusive strategies and prioritize investments in poverty eradication. Innovative solutions, robust monitoring mechanisms, and policy coherence can help accelerate progress towards ultimately ending poverty and leaving no one behind. As All We Can Methodist, we stand ready to collaborate and contribute to global efforts aimed at poverty eradication for a more sustainable and equitable future.

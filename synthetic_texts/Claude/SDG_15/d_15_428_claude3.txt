Malteser International's Analytical Report on SDG 15: Life on Land

Safeguarding terrestrial ecosystems and halting biodiversity loss is a paramount challenge that demands immediate and concerted action. As a global humanitarian organization, Malteser International recognizes the intrinsic link between environmental sustainability and human well-being, particularly for vulnerable communities in resource-constrained regions.

The ongoing degradation of forests, desertification, and loss of biodiversity pose grave threats to food security, water availability, and resilience against climate change impacts. Deforestation rates remain alarmingly high, driven by illegal logging, agricultural expansion, and unsustainable resource extraction. This not only exacerbates greenhouse gas emissions but also jeopardizes the livelihoods of indigenous populations and local communities who rely on forest resources for subsistence, traditional medicine, and cultural practices.

The extinction of species and the fragmentation of habitats disrupt intricate ecological networks, undermining nature's ability to provide vital resources and regulatory functions.

Malteser International's interventions in diverse geographic contexts have underscored the critical importance of addressing land degradation, desertification, and biodiversity loss through an integrated approach that harmonizes environmental protection with sustainable livelihood strategies. In the arid regions of the Sahel, for instance, our initiatives have focused on promoting climate-smart agriculture, agroforestry, and community-based natural resource management. By empowering local communities with knowledge and resources, we have facilitated the restoration of degraded lands, enhanced soil fertility, and improved water conservation practices.

In the Amazon basin, we have collaborated with indigenous communities to strengthen their capacity for sustainable forest management and traditional ecological knowledge. This includes supporting their efforts to secure land tenure rights, establish community-based monitoring systems, and develop sustainable income-generating activities that align with their cultural values and environmental stewardship.

Malteser International's approach to SDG 15 is grounded in the recognition that environmental degradation disproportionately impacts marginalized communities, exacerbating existing vulnerabilities and perpetuating cycles of poverty. By integrating environmental conservation with economic empowerment and capacity-building initiatives, we strive to create resilient and self-sustaining communities that can withstand environmental shocks and contribute to the long-term preservation of terrestrial ecosystems.

Effective implementation of SDG 15 requires a multi-stakeholder approach that fosters collaboration among governments, civil society organizations, private sector entities, and local communities. Malteser International actively engages in policy dialogues and advocacy efforts to promote sustainable land management practices, strengthen environmental governance frameworks, and catalyze systemic changes in resource allocation and incentive structures.

Furthermore, we recognize the importance of leveraging cutting-edge technologies and scientific research to inform our interventions. By harnessing remote sensing and geospatial analysis tools, we can monitor land cover changes, identify hotspots of degradation, and tailor our interventions accordingly. Concurrently, we collaborate with research institutions to integrate traditional ecological knowledge with modern scientific approaches, fostering cross-pollination of ideas and solutions.

As we progress towards the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, Malteser International remains steadfast in its commitment to SDG 15, recognizing that safeguarding life on land is not only an environmental imperative but also a moral obligation to ensure the well-being of present and future generations. Through our comprehensive and holistic approach, we strive to catalyze transformative change, empowering communities to become stewards of their natural resources and champions of sustainable development.

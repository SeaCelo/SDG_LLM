As an institution committed to fostering economic and social progress in the Islamic world, we acknowledge the inextricable link between the health of our oceans and the well-being of our member countries.

The ocean, a vast and interconnected ecosystem, plays a crucial role in regulating our climate, providing sustenance, and supporting countless livelihoods. However, the relentless onslaught of human activities, including overfishing, pollution, and climate change, has placed immense strain on this invaluable resource. It is our collective responsibility to address these challenges and ensure the preservation of marine ecosystems for current and future generations.

In our efforts to contribute to SDG 14, the Islamic Development Bank recognizes the need for a multifaceted approach that addresses the root causes of marine degradation while simultaneously promoting sustainable practices and innovative solutions. Firstly, we must confront the issue of overfishing, which has depleted global fish stocks to alarmingly low levels. Through targeted investments in sustainable aquaculture and the promotion of responsible fishing practices, we can alleviate the pressure on wild populations while ensuring food security and economic opportunities for coastal communities.

Furthermore, the scourge of marine pollution, particularly plastic waste, poses a grave threat to the health of our oceans. The Islamic Development Bank is committed to supporting member countries in developing comprehensive waste management systems, promoting circular economies, and raising awareness about the importance of reducing, reusing, and recycling plastics. By addressing this issue at its source, we can mitigate the devastating impacts of pollution on marine ecosystems and the food chain.

Moreover, the Islamic Development Bank recognizes the inextricable link between climate change and the well-being of our oceans. Rising sea temperatures, ocean acidification, and sea-level rise are all contributing to the degradation of coral reefs, the loss of biodiversity, and the displacement of coastal communities. We are dedicated to supporting member countries in their efforts to mitigate and adapt to the effects of climate change, through the promotion of renewable energy, sustainable infrastructure, and climate-resilient coastal development.

In addition to these efforts, the Islamic Development Bank places great emphasis on capacity building and knowledge sharing. We are committed to fostering collaboration among member countries, engaging with local communities, and promoting the exchange of best practices and innovative solutions. By leveraging the collective knowledge and expertise of our member states, we can develop tailored strategies that address the unique challenges faced by each region while ensuring the sustainable management of our shared marine resources.

Furthermore, the Islamic Development Bank recognizes the importance of marine protected areas (MPAs) in safeguarding biodiversity and preserving critical habitats. We are dedicated to supporting member countries in establishing and effectively managing MPAs, ensuring the long-term conservation of these invaluable ecosystems.

Ultimately, the Islamic Development Bank's commitment to SDG 14 is rooted in the understanding that the health of our oceans is inextricably linked to the well-being of our member countries and the global community. By fostering international cooperation, promoting sustainable practices, and investing in innovative solutions, we can protect and restore the delicate balance of our marine ecosystems, ensuring a prosperous and sustainable future for all.

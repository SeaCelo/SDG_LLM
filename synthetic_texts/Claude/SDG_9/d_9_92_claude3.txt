Rafiq Zakaria, Professor of Sustainable Development, University of Oxford

The Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 9 – "Build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization and foster innovation" – lies at the heart of the global development agenda. As a scholar with a lifetime of experience in the Global South, I have witnessed firsthand the transformative potential of this goal, as well as the monumental challenges that impede its realization.

Infrastructure, the backbone of any nation's prosperity, has long been a contentious issue in developing countries. Inadequate transportation networks, unreliable power grids, and crumbling water systems have hampered economic growth, perpetuated poverty, and exacerbated social inequalities. The lack of resilient infrastructure not only impedes productivity but also renders communities vulnerable to natural disasters and climate change impacts.

Sustainable industrialization, on the other hand, presents a delicate balancing act between economic growth and environmental stewardship. Too often, industrialization in the Global South has been pursued at the expense of ecological integrity, with factories spewing toxic emissions and depleting natural resources at an alarming rate. The challenge lies in decoupling industrial growth from environmental degradation, embracing cleaner technologies, and promoting circular economic models.

Innovation, the third pillar of SDG 9, holds the key to unlocking sustainable development pathways. Yet, the innovation landscape remains skewed, with the vast majority of research and development (R&D) investments concentrated in the Global North. This perpetuates a cycle of technological dependence, stifling the creative potential of the Global South and rendering it a mere consumer of innovations developed elsewhere.

To truly achieve SDG 9, we must confront these intertwined challenges head-on, adopting a holistic and context-specific approach. In Sub-Saharan Africa, for instance, the lack of reliable energy access remains a critical impediment to industrialization and innovation. Initiatives like the African Development Bank's Desert to Power program, which aims to harness the region's vast solar potential, offer a promising solution. By investing in renewable energy infrastructure, we can power industrial growth while mitigating environmental impacts and fostering energy security.

In South Asia, the challenge lies in managing rapid urbanization and its strain on infrastructure. Innovative approaches like the development of smart cities, which integrate digital technologies and sustainable urban planning, could pave the way for more efficient and livable urban environments. The Indian government's Smart Cities Mission, while not without its critics, represents a step in the right direction.

Across the Global South, fostering an enabling environment for innovation is paramount. This requires sustained investments in education, research, and development, as well as policies that incentivize collaboration between academia, industry, and government. Initiatives like the African Union's Science, Technology and Innovation Strategy for Africa (STISA-2024) demonstrate a commitment to nurturing homegrown innovations and fostering knowledge exchange within the continent.

Crucially, the implementation of SDG 9 must be underpinned by a strong commitment to inclusivity and equity. Infrastructure development must prioritize the needs of marginalized communities, ensuring access to essential services and economic opportunities. Industrialization must create decent work opportunities and foster local entrepreneurship, rather than perpetuating exploitative labor practices. Innovation ecosystems must be inclusive, nurturing talent from diverse backgrounds and addressing the unique challenges faced by women, youth, and other underrepresented groups.

As we navigate the complexities of SDG 9, it is essential to embrace a systems-thinking approach, recognizing the intricate interconnections between infrastructure, industrialization, and innovation. Siloed efforts will inevitably fall short, as each component reinforces and amplifies the impact of the others. By fostering cross-sectoral collaborations and adopting a holistic perspective, we can unlock synergies and catalyze transformative change.

In conclusion, SDG 9 represents a pivotal opportunity to reshape the development trajectory of the Global South. By investing in resilient infrastructure, promoting sustainable industrialization, and fostering inclusive innovation ecosystems, we can unlock a virtuous cycle of economic growth, environmental stewardship, and social progress. However, this ambitious goal requires a collective commitment from governments, private sector actors, civil society, and international organizations. It demands a willingness to challenge conventional wisdom, embrace contextually-relevant solutions, and prioritize the needs of the most vulnerable. Only through such a concerted and nuanced approach can we truly build the foundations for a more prosperous, equitable, and sustainable future for all.

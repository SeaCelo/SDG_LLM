As a leading development finance institution, SFD recognizes the pivotal role of sustainable patterns of consumption and production in achieving the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.

SDG 12 aims to decouple economic growth from environmental degradation, increase resource efficiency, and promote sustainable lifestyles. It encompasses a wide range of interconnected targets, from sustainable management of natural resources to reducing food waste, promoting sustainable public procurement practices, and raising awareness for sustainable development and lifestyles.

In the context of the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) region, where SFD operates, challenges related to SDG 12 are multifaceted. The region's economic dependence on hydrocarbons and the associated carbon-intensive industries have contributed to unsustainable consumption patterns and significant ecological footprints. Additionally, rapid urbanization, population growth, and shifting consumption habits have exacerbated resource depletion and waste generation.

Recent initiatives by GCC countries, such as the development of national strategies for sustainable consumption and production (SCP), demonstrate a growing commitment to addressing these challenges. However, effective implementation remains a formidable task, requiring a comprehensive and coordinated approach across various sectors and stakeholders.

SFD, through its financing and technical assistance programs, has been actively supporting efforts to promote sustainable consumption and production patterns in partner countries. One notable project in Yemen aimed to reduce food waste through the establishment of a pilot facility for the conversion of agricultural residues into valuable by-products, such as animal feed and compost. This initiative not only contributed to sustainable waste management but also promoted resource efficiency and supported local livelihoods.

Furthermore, SFD has been actively collaborating with regional and international organizations to facilitate knowledge sharing and capacity building on SCP practices. For instance, in partnership with the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP), SFD has supported the development of national SCP action plans in several Arab countries, providing a roadmap for integrating sustainability principles into various economic sectors.

Recognizing the importance of public-private partnerships in achieving SDG 12, SFD has also been actively engaging with private sector entities to promote sustainable business practices. One such initiative involved providing financing and technical assistance to a leading GCC-based manufacturing company to implement resource-efficient and cleaner production technologies, resulting in significant reductions in water and energy consumption, as well as greenhouse gas emissions.

While progress has been made, significant challenges remain in achieving SDG 12 in the GCC region and beyond. These include overcoming institutional and regulatory barriers, addressing data and monitoring gaps, promoting sustainable lifestyles and consumer awareness, and fostering regional and international cooperation on SCP.

As we approach the halfway mark of the 2030 Agenda, it is crucial to accelerate efforts and adopt a comprehensive and coordinated approach to responsible consumption and production. SFD remains committed to supporting partner countries in this endeavor, leveraging its financial resources, technical expertise, and strategic partnerships to drive sustainable economic growth while safeguarding the region's natural resources for future generations.

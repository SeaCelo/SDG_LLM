Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) presents a comprehensive analytical report on the current state of Sustainable Development Goal 16 (SDG 16): "Promote peaceful and inclusive societies for sustainable development, provide access to justice for all and build effective, accountable and inclusive institutions at all levels."

The pursuit of SDG 16 is a critical endeavor, as it addresses fundamental aspects of societal stability, human rights, and the rule of law â€“ essential prerequisites for sustainable development. JICA recognizes the intricate interplay between peace, justice, and strong institutions, and their profound impact on all other SDGs.

Globally, progress towards SDG 16 has been uneven and hampered by various challenges. Conflict and violence continue to plague numerous regions, resulting in loss of life, displacement, and erosion of social fabric. According to the Uppsala Conflict Data Program, the number of active conflicts worldwide has remained persistently high, with over 50 active conflicts recorded in 2022. This underscores the urgency of conflict resolution, peacebuilding, and addressing the root causes of violence.

Access to justice remains a significant hurdle, particularly for marginalized and vulnerable groups. Discriminatory laws, inadequate legal aid, and systemic barriers perpetuate inequalities and undermine the rule of law. The World Justice Project's Rule of Law Index 2022 highlights concerning trends, with over 4.4 billion people living in countries with deficient rule of law performance.

Institutional weaknesses, corruption, and lack of accountability further exacerbate these challenges. The Corruption Perceptions Index 2021 by Transparency International reveals that two-thirds of countries scored below 50 on a scale of 0 (highly corrupt) to 100 (very clean), indicating widespread corruption and lack of transparency.

JICA's interventions in support of SDG 16 are multifaceted and tailored to the specific contexts of partner countries. In conflict-affected regions, JICA prioritizes peacebuilding efforts through initiatives such as community-based reintegration programs for ex-combatants, trauma counseling, and facilitation of dialogue among stakeholders. These programs aim to address the root causes of conflict, foster reconciliation, and create an enabling environment for sustainable peace.

Strengthening the rule of law and access to justice is another key area of focus. JICA supports legal and judicial reform projects, enhancing the capacity of judicial institutions, and promoting human rights education. Innovative approaches, such as mobile legal clinics and paralegal training, are employed to reach remote and underserved communities.

Institutional capacity building is a core component of JICA's efforts, encompassing public sector reform, anti-corruption strategies, and strengthening of accountability mechanisms. Technical assistance is provided to partner governments to enhance transparency, streamline processes, and promote inclusive and participatory governance.

Furthermore, JICA recognizes the pivotal role of civil society organizations (CSOs) in advancing SDG 16. Through targeted partnerships and funding mechanisms, JICA supports CSOs in their advocacy efforts, monitoring of human rights violations, and promotion of civic engagement. These collaborations amplify the voices of marginalized groups and foster an enabling environment for civil society to thrive.

Notably, JICA's interventions are guided by a comprehensive approach that acknowledges the interdependence of SDG 16 with other SDGs. For instance, efforts to promote peaceful societies are intrinsically linked to SDG 1 (No Poverty) and SDG 4 (Quality Education), as poverty and lack of educational opportunities can fuel grievances and contribute to social unrest.

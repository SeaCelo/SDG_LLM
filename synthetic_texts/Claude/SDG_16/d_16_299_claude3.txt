Sustainable Development Goal 16: Promoting Just, Peaceful and Inclusive Societies – A Fiscal and Economic Perspective

By Alan Auerbach

The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, with its 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), represents an ambitious and comprehensive blueprint for addressing global challenges and promoting human progress. Among these goals, SDG 16 stands out as a crucial foundation for achieving sustainable development in its broadest sense. This goal, which calls for the promotion of peaceful and inclusive societies, the provision of access to justice, and the building of effective, accountable institutions, is inextricably linked to economic growth, fiscal sustainability, and overall societal well-being.

At its core, SDG 16 recognizes that lasting progress cannot be achieved in the absence of stable, just, and inclusive governance structures. Conflict, insecurity, and weak institutions breed uncertainty, undermine investment, and impede economic activity, ultimately hampering growth and development. Conversely, peaceful societies with robust legal frameworks and effective governance mechanisms create an enabling environment for commerce, innovation, and human capital accumulation.

From a fiscal perspective, the economic costs of conflict and insecurity are staggering. Armed conflicts not only divert scarce resources away from productive investments, but they also necessitate substantial expenditures on military operations, reconstruction efforts, and humanitarian aid. Moreover, the destruction of physical and human capital during conflicts represents a profound loss of productive capacity, further straining public finances and hampering long-term growth prospects.

Beyond the direct costs of conflict, weak governance and corruption can severely undermine a country's fiscal position. Ineffective tax administration, coupled with leakages and misallocation of public funds, inhibits the government's ability to mobilize domestic resources and finance essential public goods and services. Furthermore, a lack of accountability and transparency in public financial management erodes trust in institutions and deters private investment, perpetuating a vicious cycle of stagnation and fiscal strain.

Conversely, countries that have made strides in promoting peaceful, just, and inclusive societies have reaped substantial economic and fiscal dividends. For instance, consider the remarkable transformation of several East Asian economies in the latter half of the 20th century. By establishing robust legal frameworks, strengthening institutional capacity, and pursuing policies that promoted inclusive growth, these nations achieved sustained economic expansion, significant poverty reduction, and improved fiscal positions.

To illustrate the fiscal implications of SDG 16, let us consider the case of a hypothetical country grappling with internal conflict and weak governance. Suppose this nation allocates a substantial portion of its budget to military expenditures, while simultaneously experiencing significant revenue shortfalls due to ineffective tax administration and widespread corruption. In such a scenario, the government would face mounting fiscal deficits and debt, potentially leading to austerity measures that curtail essential public services and investments in human capital and infrastructure.

However, if this country were to make concerted efforts to resolve internal conflicts, strengthen the rule of law, and enhance institutional capacity, the fiscal landscape could change dramatically. A reduction in military spending, coupled with improved tax compliance and a clampdown on corruption, could generate significant fiscal savings and revenue gains. These resources could then be redirected towards productive investments in education, healthcare, and infrastructure, thereby fostering inclusive economic growth and further strengthening the government's fiscal position over the long term.

Promoting peaceful and inclusive societies necessitates addressing deep-rooted societal issues, such as inequality, marginalization, and lack of access to economic opportunities. Effective conflict resolution mechanisms, inclusive political processes, and robust institutions that uphold the rule of law are crucial components of this agenda.

Moreover, the pursuit of SDG 16 must be accompanied by complementary efforts to address other interrelated goals, such as reducing poverty (SDG 1), promoting quality education (SDG 4), and fostering economic growth and decent work (SDG 8). In conclusion, SDG 16, with its emphasis on promoting just, peaceful, and inclusive societies, represents a critical cornerstone for sustainable development and long-term economic prosperity.

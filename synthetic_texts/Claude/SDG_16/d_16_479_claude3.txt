[SDG 16: Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions]

The Washington Post
June 19, 2023

"Fostering Peace and Justice for a Sustainable Future: The Clarion Call of SDG 16"

By: Laura Kemp, Foreign Affairs Correspondent 

In our increasingly interconnected world, the pursuit of sustainable development has emerged as a critical imperative, one that transcends borders and demands a concerted global effort. At the heart of this endeavor lies the Sustainable Development Goal 16 (SDG 16), a clarion call for nations to promote peaceful and inclusive societies, ensure access to justice for all, and build effective, accountable, and inclusive institutions at all levels.

The significance of SDG 16 cannot be overstated, as it serves as a linchpin for the realization of the entire 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. Without a foundation of peace, justice, and strong institutions, the progress made in other areas such as poverty alleviation, quality education, and environmental protection risks being undermined or even reversed.

The global landscape is marred by a multitude of challenges that underscore the urgency of achieving SDG 16. Ongoing conflicts, rising authoritarianism, and systemic corruption continue to erode the fabric of societies, perpetuating cycles of violence, displacement, and human rights abuses. According to the United Nations, over 1.5 billion people live in fragile or conflict-affected contexts, with millions displaced and countless lives shattered.

Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic has exacerbated existing inequalities and strained institutional capacities worldwide, highlighting the critical need for robust and resilient governance structures. The World Justice Project's Rule of Law Index 2022 revealed concerning trends, with over 4.4 billion people living in countries where fundamental rights are not fully guaranteed, and nearly two-thirds of countries experiencing a decline in constraints on government powers.

Addressing these challenges demands a multi-faceted approach that encompasses legal and institutional reforms, capacity building, and fostering a culture of transparency and accountability. One notable initiative is the Pathfinders for Peaceful, Just and Inclusive Societies, a multi-stakeholder partnership that brings together governments, civil society organizations, and the private sector to accelerate progress towards SDG 16.

Countries like Costa Rica and New Zealand have emerged as beacons of hope, demonstrating the transformative power of strong institutions, inclusive governance, and a unwavering commitment to peace and justice. Costa Rica, for instance, has consistently ranked among the top nations in the Global Peace Index, thanks to its longstanding abolition of the military, robust social welfare policies, and proactive efforts to promote environmental sustainability.

Similarly, New Zealand's robust democratic institutions, independent judiciary, and commitment to upholding human rights have earned it a reputation as a champion of the rule of law and transparent governance. The country's innovative Well-being Budget, launched in 2019, prioritizes investments in areas that contribute to the overall well-being of its citizens, aligning with the spirit of the SDGs.

While progress has been made, the path ahead remains arduous, and complacency is not an option. Achieving SDG 16 will require sustained political will, international cooperation, and a concerted effort to address the root causes of conflict, injustice, and institutional fragility.

As global citizens, we all have a stake in fostering peaceful, just, and inclusive societies. Whether through advocacy, civic engagement, or supporting initiatives that promote transparency and accountability, each of us can contribute to the realization of SDG 16.

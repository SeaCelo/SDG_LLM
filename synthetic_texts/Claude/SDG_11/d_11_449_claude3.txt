As a global humanitarian organization committed to eradicating poverty and promoting sustainable development, Tearfund recognizes the pivotal role of cities and communities in achieving the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). SDG 11 calls for making cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable, a challenge that demands a multifaceted approach and unwavering commitment from all stakeholders.

Rapid urbanization, fueled by population growth and rural-urban migration, has led to a surge in the number of urban dwellers, with over 55% of the world's population now residing in cities. This trend is expected to continue, with projections indicating that by 2050, nearly 70% of the global population will be urban. While cities are engines of economic growth and innovation, they also face profound challenges, including inadequate housing, strained infrastructure, environmental degradation, and social inequality.

Tearfund's work in cities and communities around the world has provided us with invaluable insights into the complexities and nuances of SDG 11. We recognize that urban poverty is a multidimensional issue, encompassing not only financial deprivation but also limited access to basic services, inadequate housing, and social exclusion. In many informal settlements and slums, people live in overcrowded conditions, lacking access to clean water, sanitation, and basic healthcare, exacerbating their vulnerability to environmental hazards and health risks.

Addressing these challenges requires a holistic approach that integrates urban planning, housing policies, infrastructure development, and community engagement. Tearfund collaborates with local governments, civil society organizations, and community groups to promote inclusive and participatory urban planning processes. We advocate for pro-poor housing policies that prioritize the needs of low-income households, ensuring access to affordable and secure housing, a fundamental human right.

Furthermore, Tearfund recognizes the inextricable link between sustainable urban development and environmental sustainability. Cities are significant contributors to greenhouse gas emissions and resource depletion, but they also have the potential to drive innovation and promote sustainable practices. We work with communities to implement sustainable waste management solutions, promote energy efficiency, and encourage the use of renewable energy sources, reducing the environmental footprint of urban areas.

Resilience is a crucial aspect of SDG 11, as cities and communities must be able to withstand and recover from shocks and stresses, such as natural disasters, economic crises, and pandemics. Tearfund supports disaster risk reduction strategies, empowering communities to develop emergency preparedness plans and enhancing their capacity to respond to and recover from disasters. We also advocate for the integration of climate change adaptation measures into urban planning, ensuring that cities and communities are better equipped to cope with the impacts of climate change, such as rising sea levels, extreme weather events, and resource scarcity.

Sustainable transportation is another critical component of SDG 11, as it plays a vital role in reducing air pollution, mitigating climate change, and improving accessibility and mobility within cities. Tearfund promotes sustainable transport options, such as public transit, non-motorized transport (e.g., cycling and walking), and the use of clean energy vehicles, contributing to the development of more livable and environmentally friendly urban areas.

Crucially, Tearfund recognizes that achieving SDG 11 requires a concerted effort to address social inclusion and equity. We work to empower marginalized and vulnerable groups, such as women, youth, persons with disabilities, and refugees, ensuring their participation in urban development processes and decision-making. By promoting inclusive and participatory governance, we strive to create cities and communities that are safe, welcoming, and accessible to all.

In our pursuit of sustainable cities and communities, Tearfund employs a range of innovative approaches and partnerships. We collaborate with academic institutions and research organizations to leverage cutting-edge knowledge and best practices in urban development. We engage with the private sector to promote sustainable business practices and foster public-private partnerships that catalyze investment in sustainable infrastructure and services.

Through our work, we have witnessed the transformative power of community-led initiatives and the importance of empowering local stakeholders. We support community-driven projects that address local priorities and build upon existing knowledge and resources, ensuring that solutions are culturally appropriate and sustainable in the long term.

Achieving SDG 11 is a complex and multifaceted challenge, but Tearfund remains committed to working tirelessly towards creating inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable cities and communities. We believe that by fostering collaboration, promoting innovation, and empowering local communities, we can build a world where no one is left behind, and cities and human settlements serve as catalysts for sustainable development and human flourishing.

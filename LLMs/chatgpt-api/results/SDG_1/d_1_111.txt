Through his extensive work, Pantin has explored the multifaceted challenges of eradicating poverty and promoting inclusive economic growth. In this article, we will delve into the intricacies and implications of Sustainable Development Goal 1: No Poverty, as outlined by the United Nations.

SDG 1 seeks to end poverty in all its forms, ensuring that every individual, regardless of their location or circumstances, can access basic necessities and achieve a decent standard of living. This goal comes as a response to the persistent and deeply rooted issue of global poverty, which continues to afflict billions worldwide. To fulfill SDG 1, specific targets have been established to measure progress and guide policy interventions.

To comprehensively address poverty, SDG 1 recognizes both monetary and multidimensional dimensions of poverty. Monetarily, according to the World Bank, around 9.2 percent of the global population lives in extreme poverty, with incomes below the international poverty line of US$1.90 per day. While significant strides have been made in reducing global poverty over the past few decades, regional disparities persist, with Sub-Saharan Africa being the most affected. Therefore, one crucial aspect of SDG 1 is to economically empower individuals by creating sustainable livelihoods and promoting productive employment opportunities.

However, SDG 1 extends beyond economic indicators and encompassing a broader view of poverty as a multidimensional challenge. Poverty is not solely defined by the absence of income or assets but also by inadequate access to education, healthcare, sanitation, and other essential services. By integrating multidimensional poverty measures, such as the Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI), policymakers gain a deeper understanding of the interdependencies that perpetuate poverty cycles.

Moreover, SDG 1 emphasizes the need for targeted interventions to ensure that the progress made in poverty reduction is inclusive and leaves no one behind. It recognizes the unique vulnerabilities faced by various marginalized groups, including women, children, minorities, and people living with disabilities. Addressing gender inequality and promoting women's economic empowerment stands as a critical component of achieving SDG 1. By removing barriers that hinder women's access to resources, education, and decision-making power, societies can unlock untapped potential and foster sustainable development.

To successfully implement SDG 1, evidence-based approaches, data-driven policy decisions, and effective governance structures are indispensable. The framework promotes countries to create robust social protection systems that provide a safety net for the most vulnerable populations during times of crises or shocks. Such systems help mitigate the impacts of poverty and ensure basic needs are met, nurturing sustainable development in the face of adversity.

Furthermore, partnerships between governments, civil society organizations, private sector entities, and international institutions are crucial in mobilizing resources, sharing knowledge, and fostering collaboration to tackle poverty comprehensively. Leveraging technology and innovation is instrumental in providing efficient and scalable solutions to poverty eradication.

Interestingly, achievements in other Sustainable Development Goals are deeply interconnected with SDG 1. Progress towards education (SDG 4), healthcare (SDG 3), gender equality (SDG 5), and climate action (SDG 13) plays a fundamental role in poverty eradication efforts. Tackling poverty requires synergistic efforts across sectors and a holistic approach to systemic challenges.

In conclusion, Sustainable Development Goal 1: No Poverty aims to address global poverty by adopting a multidimensional approach to overcome barriers and disparities. By targeting both the economic and non-economic aspects of poverty, SDG 1 prioritizes the creation of sustainable livelihoods, inclusivity, and social protection systems. Moreover, it acknowledges the interconnectedness between poverty eradication and other Sustainable Development Goals, emphasizing the importance of a comprehensive and multifaceted approach. Achieving SDG 1 requires the collaboration of all stakeholders, innovative solutions, and a commitment to leave no one behind.

Renewable Energy and Sustainability: Achieving Universal Access to Affordable, Reliable and Modern Energy by 2030

James H. Stock
Professor of Economics
Harvard University

Abstract

Access to affordable, reliable, and modern energy services is an imperative for socioeconomic development. This paper explores the role of Sustainable Development Goal 7 (SDG 7) in driving the global transition towards renewable energy sources and promoting sustainability. SDG 7 seeks to ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all by 2030. It recognizes that energy is at the core of achieving a sustainable and equitable future for humanity. This article provides an overview of the various targets, indicators, and key issues related to SDG 7, and examines the progress made so far at both the national and global levels. It also discusses the challenges associated with achieving the SDG 7 targets and identifies the key policy interventions that can drive progress in this area.

Introduction

Energy plays a vital role in driving economic growth, improving access to education, healthcare, and other essential services, and eradicating poverty. However, as of 2020, an estimated 789 million people still lacked access to electricity and 2.8 billion people relied on traditional biomass for cooking and heating. Moreover, fossil fuel consumption continues to contribute to climate change and air pollution, endangering our planet's ecosystems and human health. In light of these challenges, SDG 7 serves as a comprehensive framework for addressing energy-related issues and fostering sustainable development.

Targets and Indicators

SDG 7 consists of five targets that cover the key dimensions of energy access, renewables, and energy efficiency. The targets are interrelated and mutually supportive. The first target aims to ensure universal access to affordable and reliable electricity services. It measures the proportion of the population with access to electricity and encourages countries to invest in infrastructure and technologies that promote affordable and reliable energy services.

The second and third targets focus on expanding access to clean cooking fuels and technologies. They measure the proportion of the population relying on traditional biomass and monitor progress in the adoption of cleaner and more efficient cooking solutions.

The fourth target calls for substantial increases in the global share of renewable energy in the overall energy mix, while the fifth encourages policy frameworks and financial incentives to enhance energy efficiency. These targets recognize that promoting renewable energy sources and improving energy efficiency are crucial for reducing greenhouse gas emissions, mitigating climate change, and ensuring a sustainable energy transition.

Progress and Challenges

The global efforts towards achieving SDG 7 have yielded positive results. Between 2010 and 2018, the global population without access to electricity decreased from 1.2 billion to 789 million. Nevertheless, significant challenges persist, particularly in sub-Saharan Africa and Southern Asia, where progress has been slow due to factors such as inadequate infrastructure, limited financial resources, and political instability.

Expanding access to clean cooking solutions remains a formidable challenge. Despite some progress, the number of people relying on traditional biomass for cooking actually increased between 2014 and 2020 due to population growth. This indicates the need for accelerated efforts to transition towards cleaner cooking options and sustainable practices.

Furthermore, while the use of renewable energy has increased significantly in recent years, it still accounts for only approximately 17% of global final energy consumption. Scaling up investments and adopting supportive policies are necessary to accelerate this transition. Similarly, energy efficiency improvements require widespread adoption of efficient technologies, coupled with supportive regulations and market mechanisms.

Policy Interventions

The achievement of SDG 7 requires a combination of policy interventions spanning multiple domains. National governments play a crucial role in creating an enabling environment by setting clear targets and regulations, providing financial incentives, and promoting public-private partnerships. They should also prioritize investments in energy infrastructure, particularly in underserved areas.

International cooperation is essential for driving progress, particularly in the form of technology transfer, capacity building, and financial support. Developing countries often lack the necessary resources and expertise to implement sustainable energy projects at scale. Developed countries can support their efforts by providing financial assistance, technical expertise, and sharing best practices.

Conclusion

SDG 7 provides a comprehensive roadmap for achieving universal access to affordable, reliable, and modern energy by 2030. It addresses the interconnectedness of energy access, renewables, and energy efficiency, emphasizing the need for sustainable practices. While progress has been made, concerted efforts are still required to overcome challenges and accelerate the global transition towards a sustainable energy future. This will require a combination of policy interventions, financial investments, technological advancements, and international cooperation. By prioritizing SDG 7, we can not only ensure a brighter and more equitable future for all, but also mitigate the pressing issues of climate change and environmental degradation facing our planet today.

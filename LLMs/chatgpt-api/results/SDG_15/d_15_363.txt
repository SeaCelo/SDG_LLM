Overview of SDG 15:
SDG 15 calls for the protection, restoration, and sustainable management of terrestrial ecosystems, forests, and biodiversity. With nine targets, this goal aims to halt deforestation, prevent land degradation, conserve biodiversity, and contribute to climate change mitigation and adaptation.

Progress and Challenges:
Significant progress has been made towards achieving SDG 15 since its adoption in 2015. International efforts, such as the Bonn Challenge and the New York Declaration on Forests, have played a key role in mobilizing action and increasing commitments to forest restoration. However, challenges still persist, especially in countries with high rates of deforestation and unsustainable land use practices.

Forest cover loss continues at an alarming rate, primarily driven by conversion to agriculture, unsustainable logging, and infrastructure development. Deforestation contributes to climate change, biodiversity loss, and the erosion of ecosystem services, exacerbating existing environmental and social challenges.

Furthermore, the loss of biodiversity, including the decline of species populations and the degradation of ecosystems, poses critical threats to the functioning of ecosystems and the services they provide. Unchecked expansion of agricultural activities, illegal wildlife trade, and habitat destruction contribute significantly to this issue.

Recommendations for Action:
To effectively address the challenges posed by SDG 15, enhanced cooperation and collaboration among stakeholders is paramount. The EBRD recommends a multifaceted approach that encompasses policy interventions, financial support mechanisms, and capacity building efforts.

1. Policy Interventions: Governments and international organizations need to enact and enforce policies aimed at reducing deforestation, promoting sustainable land use practices, and conserving biodiversity. These policies should be backed by legal frameworks, robust monitoring systems, and adequate penalties for non-compliance. Sustainability principles should be integrated into land-use planning, agricultural subsidies, and infrastructure development processes.

2. Financial Support Mechanisms: Mobilizing sufficient and sustainable financial resources is crucial to achieve SDG 15 targets. The EBRD encourages increased investment in sustainable forestry and agroforestry practices, as well as the development of climate-smart agriculture. Innovative financial instruments, such as green bonds and impact investment funds, can attract private sector participation and incentivize sustainable land management.

3. Capacity Building: Strengthening the capacity of local communities, landowners, and relevant stakeholders is essential for sustainable land management. Knowledge transfer on sustainable land use practices, biodiversity conservation, and reforestation techniques should be a priority. This can be achieved through partnerships, training programs, and the dissemination of best practices.

Furthermore, partnerships between governments, civil society organizations, and the private sector are critical to prioritize SDG 15 implementation. Collaboration in research, technology transfer, and the sharing of knowledge and expertise will foster innovation in tackling key challenges related to land conservation and ecosystem restoration.

Conclusion:
The achievement of SDG 15 requires a concerted effort on a global scale to protect terrestrial ecosystems, conserve biodiversity, and reverse the current trends of deforestation and land degradation. The EBRD remains committed to supporting countries in their efforts towards sustainable land management through financial investments, partnerships, and the sharing of best practices. By implementing the recommended actions, we can work together to safeguard the crucial natural resources and ecosystems that sustain life on land for present and future generations.

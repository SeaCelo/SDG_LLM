Abstract:
This article aims to examine the role of Sustainable Development Goal 3 (SDG 3) in promoting global health and wellbeing. SDG 3, entitled "Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages," is one of the seventeen Sustainable Development Goals adopted by United Nations member states in 2015. This paper delves into the background and scope of SDG 3, analyzes its key targets and indicators, discusses progress made towards achieving the goal, and explores challenges faced in its implementation. Additionally, it evaluates the potential impact of SDG 3 on socioeconomic development in different regions of the world. Finally, leveraging empirical evidence and case studies, this article highlights the importance of international cooperation and policy coherence for effectively attaining SDG 3.

1. Introduction
SDG 3 is a fundamental pillar of the United Nations' agenda for addressing global challenges and ensuring sustainable wellbeing for everyone. Its multi-dimensional approach takes into account various health issues and their interconnectedness with social, economic, and environmental factors. This article critically assesses the progress of SDG 3 towards accomplishing its targets and discusses challenges encountered during implementation.

2. Background and Scope of SDG 3
SDG 3 encompasses a broad range of health-related targets, such as maternal and child health, infectious diseases, non-communicable diseases, mental health, and access to essential medicines. It recognizes the cross-cutting nature of health and its crucial role in achieving sustainable development globally. By prioritizing preventive healthcare, healthcare services' accessibility and affordability, and achieving universal health coverage, SDG 3 equips countries with a holistic framework for improving health outcomes.

3. Targets and Indicators of SDG 3
The article further examines the specific targets and indicators set within SDG 3. These include reducing global maternal mortality, ending preventable deaths of newborns and children under five, combating epidemics such as HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, and malaria, promoting mental health and well-being, and strengthening health systems globally. Each target is accompanied by an array of indicators that allow for monitoring progress and identifying areas of improvement.

4. Progress and Challenges
This section discusses the progress made towards achieving SDG 3 and highlights exemplary initiatives implemented in various regions of the world. It commends successful efforts such as reducing global under-five mortality rates, expanding immunization coverage, promoting sexual and reproductive health, and tackling the burden of non-communicable diseases. However, it also acknowledges the persistent challenges faced in reaching the targets of SDG 3, particularly in low-income countries with limited resources and fragile health systems.

5. Impact on Socioeconomic Development
To evaluate the potential impact of SDG 3 on socioeconomic development, this section explores current research and empirical evidence. It demonstrates the interconnectedness between health and economic progress, showcasing the positive effects of investments in health infrastructure, disease prevention, and health promotion. By reducing the disease burden and ensuring healthier populations, countries are poised for increased productivity, better educational outcomes, and enhanced human capital.

6. International Cooperation and Policy Coherence
Recognizing the significance of international cooperation and policy coherence, this section emphasizes the need for collective action in achieving SDG 3. It stresses the importance of coordinating efforts across various sectors and stakeholders, including governments, civil society organizations, private sector entities, and international organizations. The article also examines the role of innovative financing mechanisms, technology transfer, and capacity-building initiatives in supporting diverse health-related interventions.

7. Conclusion
In conclusion, SDG 3 plays a critical role in promoting global health and wellbeing. This article highlights its multifaceted targets and indicators, commending positive progress while addressing persistent challenges. Unlocking the potential of SDG 3 requires concerted efforts from all actors, ensuring policies are aligned, resources are mobilized, and health systems effectively respond to the evolving public health landscape. By adopting a comprehensive and inclusive approach to health, SDG 3 becomes a catalyst for sustainable development in all regions of the world.

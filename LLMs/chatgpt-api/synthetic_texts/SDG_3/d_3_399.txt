BRAC International
Analytical Report: Achieving Sustainable Development Goal 3: Ensure Healthy Lives and Promote Well-being for All at All Ages

Introduction
As the global community continues its pursuit of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, Sustainable Development Goal 3 (SDG 3) plays a crucial role in improving global health outcomes and promoting well-being for all. BRAC, dedicated to enabling people to realize their potential and promoting positive change, is committed to the achievement of SDG 3. This report aims to provide an in-depth analysis of the current status of SDG 3 targets and indicators, examining the progress made, challenges faced, and potential strategies to ensure healthy lives and well-being for all at all ages.

Target 3.1: Reduce Maternal Mortality
The reduction of maternal mortality has been a critical issue on the global agenda. While substantial progress has been made in recent years, a deeper analysis reveals that significant regional disparities still persist. For example, Sub-Saharan Africa and Southern Asia continue to bear the highest burden, primarily due to inadequate access to quality maternal health services. To address this, BRAC has been working relentlessly to improve maternal and reproductive healthcare, focusing on community-based awareness, access to skilled birth attendants, and the provision of essential health supplies.

Target 3.2: End Preventable Deaths of Newborns and Children Under 5 Years of Age
Efforts to reduce child mortality have resulted in noticeable gains, with global under-five mortality rate halving since 1990. However, complications arising from preterm birth, pneumonia, malaria, and malnutrition remain key challenges. In response, BRAC has implemented a range of interventions, including community health worker programs, essential newborn care training, and community-based management of childhood illnesses. Collaborative efforts with governments and international partners aim to scale up these proven approaches to enhance child survival and ensure national health systems' resilience.

Target 3.3: Combat Communicable Diseases
Significant progress has been achieved in combatting communicable diseases such as HIV/AIDS, malaria, and tuberculosis. However, persistent challenges include limited access to services, treatment adherence, and emerging treatment-resistant strains. BRAC continually invests in community-level initiatives, ensuring information dissemination, prevention, treatment, and support for affected individuals and communities. By strengthening health systems and promoting community engagement, the fight against communicable diseases can be further accelerated.

Target 3.4: Reduce Non-Communicable Diseases & Promote Mental Health
Non-communicable diseases (NCDs), including cardiovascular diseases, cancer, diabetes, and mental health disorders, are now responsible for a significant proportion of global morbidity and mortality. In response, BRAC emphasizes the integration of NCD prevention, awareness, and management into primary healthcare systems, addressing risk factors like tobacco use, unhealthy diet, physical inactivity, and harmful use of alcohol. Strengthening mental healthcare services through community-based initiatives, reducing stigma, and fostering social support networks are essential in addressing the mental health burden.

Target 3.5: Strengthen Prevention and Treatment of Substance Abuse
Substance abuse, including drug addiction and alcohol consumption, poses a substantial burden on societies worldwide, with severe implications for health, social well-being, and productivity. BRAC recognizes the criticality of holistic approaches which combine awareness, prevention, and treatment to address the multi-dimensional nature of substance abuse. Collaborative efforts with government agencies, NGOs, and local communities contribute towards formulating evidence-based strategies, advancing policy reforms, and expanding harm reduction interventions.

Target 3.6: Halve the Number of Road Traffic Injuries and Deaths
Road traffic injuries remain a significant global health concern, leading to preventable deaths, disabilities, and socio-economic hardships. To combat this, BRAC prioritizes road safety awareness campaigns, provides first-aid training, and advocates for robust policy frameworks and improved infrastructural designs. Collaborating with local governments and international partners, BRAC endeavors to reduce road traffic injuries and promote safer transportation systems to save lives.

Conclusion
While commendable progress has been made towards achieving SDG 3, the challenges that persist necessitate sustained efforts and innovative approaches. BRAC, under its holistic health programs, continues to pursue transformative strategies to address health inequalities, enhance health systems, and advance well-being across communities. Long-term success depends on strengthening partnerships, engaging diverse stakeholders, leveraging technology for healthcare innovations, and ensuring a gender-responsive approach to promote healthy lives and well-being for all at all ages. By persevering in these efforts, we can build a more resilient and equitable world where fundamental rights to health are enjoyed by every individual, leaving no one behind.
